# Curated Reading List: AI PM Foundations

## About This List

A starting point for PMs building foundational literacy in AI and generative AI. Curated to prioritise accessible, PM-relevant resources over technical depth.

Last updated: 2026-05-01 | Level: Beginner to Intermediate

*Found a stale link or want to add a resource? Open a PR or Issue.*

---

## Papers and Technical Reads (Accessible to Non-Engineers)

| Title | Author / Source | Year | PM Takeaway |
|-------|----------------|------|-------------|
| Attention Is All You Need | Vaswani et al. (Google) | 2017 | The transformer architecture underpins all modern LLMs. You don't need to read the math — understanding the abstract is enough for a PM. |
| GPT-4 Technical Report | OpenAI | 2023 | Covers how GPT-4 was evaluated, including the capability benchmarks that inform how providers talk about model quality. |
| Sparks of Artificial General Intelligence | Microsoft Research | 2023 | An early exploration of emergent capabilities in GPT-4. Useful mental model for "why does the model sometimes surprise you?" |
| Constitutional AI | Anthropic | 2022 | Explains how AI safety constraints are built into model training. Background for PMs building products on safety-focused models. |

---

## Industry Reports (Annual / Regular)

| Title | Publisher | Cadence | PM Takeaway |
|-------|-----------|---------|-------------|
| State of AI Report | Nathan Benaich / Air Street Capital | Annual (Oct) | The best annual macro view of what's actually shipping vs. what's hype. Essential for roadmap context. |
| McKinsey Global Survey on AI | McKinsey | Annual | Tracks enterprise AI adoption patterns. Useful for understanding where your buyers/users are on the adoption curve. |
| AI Index Report | Stanford HAI | Annual | Academic, but the "deployment" sections are useful for PMs who need data to back up AI product decisions. |

## Essential Blog Posts and Articles

| Title | Author | PM Takeaway |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| The Bitter Lesson | Rich Sutton | Understanding why scaling beats clever architecture has implications for how to think about AI capability trajectories. |
| Alignment Tax | Various | Useful framing for why safer AI sometimes means less capable AI — a real product trade-off. |
| Why Chatbots Are Not the Future | (Search for this title) | Useful counterweight to chat-interface hype — most user needs are better served by non-conversational AI. |

## Books

| Title | Author | PM Takeaway |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| Co-Intelligence: Living and Working with AI | Ethan Mollick | Practical, grounded take on working with AI. Most accessible book for PMs entering the space. |
| The Alignment Problem | Brian Christian | Background on AI safety concerns. Important context for PMs building high-stakes AI products. |
| Power and Prediction | Ajay Agrawal, Joshua Gans, Avi Goldfarb | Economic framing of AI as a "prediction machine." Helps PMs think about where AI creates value. |
