# Playbook: Launching an AI Feature

## Trigger

Use this playbook when you are preparing to ship a feature that includes AI-generated content, AI-assisted decisions, or any LLM-powered functionality to end users.

**This is different from a standard feature launch because:**
- The output is probabilistic — you can't fully QA it before launch
- Failure modes may not surface until real users with diverse inputs use it
- User trust is harder to rebuild after an AI failure than a traditional bug

---

## Phase 1: Pre-Launch Preparation (T-2 weeks)

**Step 1: Define the success bar**

Before launch, write down exactly what "good enough" looks like for AI output quality. Be specific:
- What is the minimum acceptable acceptance rate?
- What failure modes are launch-blocking vs. acceptable post-launch?
- Who is the decision-maker if quality is borderline at launch?

**Step 2: Red-team the feature**

Run adversarial testing with inputs designed to break the model:
- Edge cases (empty input, very long input, input in other languages)
- Sensitive content (does the model handle it appropriately?)
- Competitor or legally sensitive mentions
- Inputs that are ambiguous or contradictory

Document every failure mode found. Decide which are launch-blocking and fix them.

**Step 3: Draft the AI behaviour disclosure**

Users should know when AI is involved in generating content or making decisions. Draft the in-product language that communicates this — even a small "Generated by AI" label sets appropriate expectations and reduces trust damage when errors occur.

**Step 4: Set up the human review pipeline**

For the first 2–4 weeks post-launch, plan to have a human sample and review AI outputs. Define:
- What % of outputs will be sampled?
- Who does the review?
- Where do issues get logged?
- What is the escalation path?

---

## Phase 2: Staged Rollout (T-0)

**Step 5: Start with internal dogfooding**

Ship to internal users only for at least 48 hours. Get structured feedback on quality, not just "does it work."

**Step 6: Design your rollout gates**

Define the criteria that unlock each rollout stage:

| Stage | Audience | Gate Criteria |
|-------|----------|---------------|
| 0 — Dogfood | Internal team | No launch-blocking bugs |
| 1 — Alpha | 1–5% of users | Acceptance rate > [X]%, no critical failures |
| 2 — Beta | 10–20% of users | Retention delta positive, quality stable |
| 3 — GA | All users | All metrics within target ranges |

**Step 7: Define the rollback trigger**

Before launch, agree on exactly what metric or event would trigger an immediate rollback. Write it down. Having this agreed in advance removes the hard conversation in the middle of an incident.

---

## Phase 3: Post-Launch Monitoring (T+0 to T+14)

**Step 8: Monitor the right signals in the first 48 hours**

- Acceptance rate (are users using the AI output, or ignoring it?)
- Edit rate (are users heavily editing AI output? High edit rate = quality problem)
- Fallback rate (are users abandoning the AI path?)
- Error rate and latency
- User-initiated support tickets mentioning the AI feature

**Step 9: Two-week retrospective**

At the two-week mark, run a focused review:
- Quality metrics vs. targets
- Unexpected failure modes discovered
- User feedback themes
- Cost per query vs. budget

Decide: continue, iterate, or roll back.

---

## Outputs

At the end of this playbook you should have:
- [ ] Documented success bar
- [ ] Red-team findings log
- [ ] In-product AI disclosure copy
- [ ] Human review process running
- [ ] Rollout gate criteria documented
- [ ] Rollback trigger agreed
- [ ] Two-week retro scheduled

## Common Gotchas

- **Not defining "good enough" before launch.** Leads to arguments about quality at launch time when it's too late to do anything about it.
- **Skipping the red-team.** Real users will find failure modes within hours of launch.
- **No rollback plan.** If something goes wrong and you haven't pre-agreed the rollback trigger, you'll lose time to debate.
- **Shipping without AI disclosure.** Even a small label sets expectations and protects user trust.
